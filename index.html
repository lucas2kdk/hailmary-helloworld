RETURNING

The Petrova Line dimmed in the spring of '35.

For eleven years we watched a single organism — microscopic, radiation-sweet, and unimaginably hungry — feed itself on the body of our sun. Models converged on a single conclusion: there would be no second century for our civilization unless we found, at Tau Ceti, the reason that one nearby star had refused to die.

We sent a teacher because a teacher does not believe in unsolvable problems. We sent him alone because we could not afford the weight of hope. The signal printed below is what came back, four words at a time, after he found someone else out there who was also not ready to give up.
